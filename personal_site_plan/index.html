Overview

Purpose

An informational website can be used to provide visitors with information about various crafts, such as how to make them or where to buy supplies. The main purpose of the informational website is to help users to find the necessary information on a specific topic

Audience

People who like handcrafted/unique items: This segment of the target market for a craft business includes people who are specifically interested in handcrafted items People who are looking for a specific item that they cannot find in stores: This segment includes people who are looking for unique or hard-to-find items People who like to support small businesses or local businesses: This segment includes people who prefer to buy from small or local businesses rather than large corporation

Content

Home page

People can see the website name and a banner at the top of this page. As you scroll down, you will find a grid with 3 card-like articles, detailing how our service works using the tag manager, analytics and ands tools. Some of the questions you might have include:

  • Why is it important to showcase my crafts to the world?
  • My craft workshop is small, why should I compete with the larger ones?
  • How do I create a quick craft project with standout results?
  • Is the era of paper and cardboard in crafts over?
  • What's the point of managing my materials well?
  • Are my tools and techniques in line with current craft trends?
  • How do I update my craft project plan?

    • My website offers guidance from a team of experts in various areas of craft techniques, materials, and design. We provide direct support for craft platforms such as Etsy, Craftsy, Handmade at Amazon, etc

    Images for the Home page

    FAQ

    As users scroll down, they will see 6 frequently asked questions about craft techniques, materials, or design, along with a headline and a brief description at the top. There is no tutorial on this page; only answers to frequently asked questions that don't require step-by-step guidance. Some of the questions they might have:

  • When should I update my craft project designs?
  • How can I tell if my crafting technique is up-to-date?
  • How can I determine if a crafting technique will suit my project?
  • Which is better for my craft store, Etsy or Handmade at Amazon?
  • How can I use craft tutorials to improve my crafting skills?
  • How can I get help with my craft projects?

    • My clients can find answers to the most frequently asked questions on this FAQ page of the website. There is no need for a tutorial for any of the questions, and they are not listed on the home page. My clients will visit this page to find out more information and get answers to some of their questions that the home page cannot address. Additionally, they will learn about the craft services I offer and whether they can visit my workshop in person or contact me online for assistance.

    Images for the FAQ page

    About Us

    This content features images that showcase my craftwork and related projects (stock internet photos), paired with two key questions and a concise paragraph addressing each question, as visitors scroll down the page. The goal is to offer quick and captivating insights into my crafting services. Potential clients will have the chance to explore fundamental aspects of my craft business, such as my workshop location, the physical address of my crafting studio, my years of experience in the craft world, and specifics about my craft education and training. Visitors are drawn to this page aiming to verify the authenticity and quality of my crafting enterprise, to learn more about my background, and to discern where they can seek assistance for their crafting endeavors.

  • Is this craftwork genuinely handmade?
  • Why should I trust this craftsperson's advice and designs?
  • Where can I learn more about craft techniques and trends?
  • Where do I go to join craft workshops or classes?
  • What's the studio address to visit and see the crafts in person?
